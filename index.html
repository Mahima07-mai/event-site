All that you need to know about Hack4Safe ThanjAI

ThanjAI is an innovative hackathon organized by SASTRA in association with the Thanjai Police in order to foster development of tools and technology. The domain for the hackathon is Cyber Security. The hackathon will be held in a hybrid form with the initial idea submission round being online and the grand finale at SASTRA Thanjavur campus.

The hackathon aims to bring together the brightest minds and focus their efforts on building the tech of tomorrow and helping serve society.

Guidelines:

  • The hackathon will have two stages.
  • Registration will be open till 25.01.2026 (23:59 IST).
  • Registration is open to all Indian citizens and is free of charge.
  • The initial idea submission (Phase 1) round will accept submissions during the window 28.01.2026, 08:00 IST to 18.02.2026, 23:59 IST.
  • Phase 1 results will be announced tentatively on 01.03.2026
  • The grand finale (for shortlisted participants) will be held on 14.03.2026 and 15.03.2026 at SASTRA Campus.
  • Details regarding the finale will be intimated to shortlisted participants over mail.

Rules:

  • Respect: Participants should respect each other, the judges, mentors, and organizers. Any form of discrimination, harassment, or bullying will not be tolerated
  • Original Work: Participants must create original work during the hackathon. Copying or plagiarism is not allowed and may result in disqualification
  • Use of Resources: Participants can use open-source code and libraries but should not use code or materials that they do not have the right to use or distribute
  • Code Quality: All code must be well-documented and maintainable. Participants should avoid hacks and shortcuts that could compromise the quality and security of their project
  • Teamwork: Teams should work collaboratively and fairly. Any attempt to sabotage or disrupt another team's work will not be tolerated
  • Presentation: Teams must present their project to the judges within the given time limit. Presentations should be clear, concise, and engaging
  • Safety: Participants must follow safety guidelines when using hardware, software, or any other equipment during the hackathon
  • Code Ownership: Participants own the intellectual property rights to their projects, but they must also make their code open source and available to others
  • Time Management: Participants should manage their time effectively to ensure that they complete their project within the given time limit
  • Feedback: Participants should be open to feedback and constructive criticism from judges.
  • Final Authority: Decisions made by judges and the organizing committee will be at their discretion and will be treated as final

Stages and Timelines

28 Jan 26

Idea Submission Round (Phase 1)

In this round, we expect you to submit two things - a PowerPoint presentation and a pitch video. The PowerPoint presentation should include your problem statement, your solution, and the impact and USP of your solution. Be terse in the ppt, and save the details for the pitch video. The video is expected to be 1-3 minutes long and should explain your product in detail. Please ensure that both the ppt and the video are uploaded as publicly available before you share the links with us.
